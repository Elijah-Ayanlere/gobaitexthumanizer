What is AI detection?

AI detection technologies use advanced machine learning models to spot content generated by AI. The primary function of an AI detector is to identify certain features within the text that distinguish it from human writing. These detectors often rely on language models similar to the ones used by AI writing tools. They analyze two main aspects: perplexity and burstiness. Perplexity measures the unpredictability of content, or how likely it is to confuse a reader. AI-generated content typically has low perplexity, meaning it's coherent and flows well, but it's predictable. Human writing, on the other hand, tends to have higher perplexity due to more varied language use. Burstiness refers to the variation in sentence structure and length. Text with little variation in these areas exhibits low burstiness, which is a sign of AI-generated content. AI tools often produce content with predictable sentence lengths and structures, whereas human writing is more dynamic. When content shows low burstiness, it's more likely to be AI-generated.

How do AI detectors work?

AI detection software uses the same language models as AI-powered content creation tools. It employs ML (machine learning) and NLP (natural language processing) and artificial intelligence technology to analyze content and identify patterns. Each detector is trained on massive datasets of human-written and AI-generated texts, with its algorithm working on the assumption that content produced by artificial intelligence has certain features, such as repetitiveness, lack of semantic meaning, conventional word patterns, etc. The detector analyzes content for a range of parameters, including wording, sentence structures, and predictability, and compares it with the database of texts produced by humans and artificial intelligence.

Can I trust AI detection tools today?

An artificial intelligence content detector typically provides a score showing the likeliness of the text being produced by AI. The accuracy of detection tools varies depending on the tool and the content complexity. Even the most advanced AI detectors aren't foolproof and sometimes provide false results. It's important to understand that the detectors aren't equal. They can utilize different models, leading to different results. Moreover, some detectors work better than others, and it often happens that detectors that generally work well have issues with identifying content produced by both a human and artificial intelligence. Given that, you can't entirely rely on AI detectors, making bypass AI detection tools an effective solution.
